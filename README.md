Employee attrition represents a severe operational and economic challenge for 
large enterprises, resulting in substantial financial losses and degraded institutional productivity 
. This project investigates the structural drivers of workforce turnover by analyzing a 
comprehensive human resources dataset containing 14,999 employee records across ten multidimensional features. We implemented an advanced analytical pipeline encompassing detailed 
statistical profiling, non-linear bivariate analysis, and K-Means clustering to uncover hidden 
workforce personas. Subsequently, two distinct machine learning paradigms were developed 
and optimized: a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) Artificial Neural Network trained via the BackPropagation algorithm, and an ensemble Gradient Boosting Classifier. On a stratified 20% 
validation hold-out set, the MLP framework achieved an accuracy of 92.57% and an F1-score of 
84.46%, whereas the Gradient Boosting Classifier demonstrably outperformed the neural 
network by securing an overall accuracy of 97.37%, an F1-score of 94.30%, and a critical recall 
rate of 92.49%. These empirical results provide corporate executive leadership with both a 
highly precise predictive risk-assessment tool and an actionable behavioral roadmap necessary 
to proactively design and execute targeted talent retention strategies.
